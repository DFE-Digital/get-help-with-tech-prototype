Get internet access

Broadband for families who receive Universal Credit

Virgin media (opens in a new tab) is providing broadband for £15 per month for families who receive Universal Credit.

Request internet access now

As soon as your school experiences a disruption to face-to-face education, we’ll aim to provide the connectivity you’ve requested. Having some information ahead of time will help us move faster to give your pupils what they need.

Request internet access now for each pupil who does not have:

  • fixed broadband at home
  • a mobile phone with enough data

We can increase a pupil’s (or a family member’s) mobile phone data allowance if they are on a participating network.

If they do not have access to a mobile phone, or is not on a participating network, we can provide a wireless 4G router.

Follow these steps now

1. Collect the information you’ll need to request internet access

You’ll need to find out if the pupil has access to a phone (or multiple phones) they can use for tethering to connect to the internet and access remote learning.

For each phone number a {{ data.setting.learner }} uses, we need:

  • the account holder’s name
  • their mobile number (beginning with ‘07’)
  • their mobile network
  • to know whether they pay monthly or Pay-as-you-go

2. Share the privacy policy with the account holder

Before you add any personal information to this service, we want you to share the DfE privacy policy with the account holder. It explains how the mobile network will use the account holder’s data.
